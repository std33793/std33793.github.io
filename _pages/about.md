Sumin Gim is a first year Ph.D. student in the Department of English at the University of Miami, working in the field of nineteenth and twentieth-century American novels and plays. Her research investigates the profound effects of technological developments, especially artificial light, on the literature, culture, individuals and society of the period.

## Detailed Research Description 
* Her current research specifically explores how technology developed in the nineteenth century impacted American society, individuals, and literature in the twentieth century. The invention of artificial light, particularly gas light in the nineteenth century, not only altered daily life and network system in America but also redefined how Americans perceived time and space. These advancements also led to psychological confusion. Beyond its physical function, gas light used as a literary and theatrical device, influencing characters’ psychology and reflecting societal anxieties. Her research examines the gas light technology as a literary device that mirrors individual psychology. She also aims to utilize gas light not only as a literary device but also as a means to understand broader societal contexts in the nineteenth and twentieth century America.
