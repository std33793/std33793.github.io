## DH Project (Expected)

* Tentative Title: Illuminating "Light": Light Symbolism in Charles Dickens's A Christmas Carol and Great Expectations
* Abstract
: This project seeks to explore the ways in which the “light” metaphor is used and its implications in Charles Dickens’s Great Expectations (1861) and A Christmas Carol (1843). It aims to visualize repetitive patterns related to the keyword “light” by utilizing topic modeling and computer-based text analysis techniques. Based on this, it intends to analyze in-depth the subtle semantic differences and contextual meanings of keywords within the patterns through a close reading. As a specific research method, I utilize topic modeling techniques to analyze the frequency and correlation of keywords related to “light.” First, the text of Dickens’s work, provided by the Gutenberg Project, will be uploaded to Voyant Tools for basic visualization and word frequency analysis. Next, I will use MALLET to process stop word filtering and then apply the LDA (Latent Dirichlet Allocation) algorithm to classify co-occurring words into topics and visualize them. However, since the analysis results from digital tools may have limitations in grasping the complex semantic structure of words in the text, the humanities research methodology of close reading will be performed in parallel, based on the results of the digital tool analysis. Through this, I plan to add depth to the research by deeply analyzing metaphorical expressions and contextual meanings that are difficult to capture only through visualization. In the long run, this project will expand its analysis to other works by Dickens and use the accumulated data to lay the groundwork for Dickens researchers to explore in-depth the changes in the “light” metaphor throughout his work and the connection between nineteenth-century British society and Dickens’s works.
